# Krosis — Gameplay Guide

## Before You Start

After creating a new character, **do not move, press any keys, open menus, or interact with anything**.

Krosis needs some time to initialize its systems and build the Legacy of the Dragonborn museum displays. Wait until the **museum build notification** appears on the screen.

Only after this notification has appeared should you begin playing.

# ORomance Plus

ORomance Plus replaces Skyrim’s simplistic relationship system with a more involved social and romantic framework.

NPCs have individual preferences and personalities. Some may be interested in casual relationships, some prefer commitment, some are monogamous, and others may not be attracted to your character at all.

## Opening the ORomance Menu

Face an NPC and press the ORomance interaction key.

The default key is:

**K — Open ORomance interaction**

Inside the menu:

* use the left and right movement keys to switch options;
* press the normal Activate key to confirm;
* press the usual menu-back key to close the interface.

The keybind can be changed in the ORomance Plus MCM.

## Building Relationships

Do not expect every interaction to succeed immediately.

The most effective ways to improve your chances are:

* complete quests for the NPC;
* travel with them if they are a follower;
* improve your Speech skill;
* increase your character level;
* become a thane;
* purchase property;
* perform major heroic deeds.

Completing an NPC’s quest is usually one of the strongest ways to improve their opinion of you.

Low-level characters are expected to fail frequently. It is recommended to reach approximately **level 5 and Speech 35** before seriously engaging with the system. Speech 50 makes romance considerably more manageable.

## Available Interactions

The menu can contain actions such as:

### Inquire

Allows you to learn more about an NPC:

* ask whether they are single or married;
* ask about their romantic preferences;
* ask about their experience;
* get hints about their personality.

An NPC may refuse to answer personal questions if they barely know you.

### Compliment

Improves an NPC’s opinion when successful.

Compliments cannot simply be spammed. Social interactions generally have cooldowns, and repeating a failed action immediately will not change the result.

### Give Gift

Gifts temporarily improve an NPC’s opinion.

Expensive gifts do not automatically guarantee success, especially when given to strangers. Gifts are more useful when a relationship has already begun to develop.

### Confess Love

Asks the NPC to begin a committed relationship with you.

Some NPCs strongly prefer a relationship before accepting intimate interactions. Others may be more interested in something casual.

### Seduce

Attempts to initiate an intimate encounter.

If the attempt succeeds, the NPC will follow you. Move to a suitable private location and open the ORomance menu on them again to begin the OStim scene.

### Kiss

Kissing can significantly improve an established relationship. The animation itself is handled through OStim.

### Propose Marriage

Marriage is no longer unlocked merely by wearing an Amulet of Mara.

The NPC must know and like you, generally be in a relationship with you, and be willing to accept the proposal.

ORomance Plus supports divorce, breakups, expanded wedding mechanics, and multiple spouses. However, NPCs can still react negatively to infidelity depending on their personality.

## Success Indicators

Once the NPC is your partner, or your Speech skill reaches 50, the interface can provide an approximate indication of whether an action will succeed.

* **Red:** the action will fail.
* **Yellow:** the NPC is close, but the action may still fail.
* **Green:** the action will succeed.

There is no random success roll.

Reloading the game or repeatedly selecting the same failed option will not produce a different result. Improve the NPC’s opinion, your Speech, your level, or another relevant condition before trying again.

## Jealousy and Infidelity

You may have several relationships, but this does not mean every partner will accept them.

If a partner catches you being intimate with another NPC, they may:

* become angry;
* refuse to interact;
* demand an apology;
* end the relationship.

Some personalities are more tolerant, but genuinely non-monogamous NPCs are relatively uncommon.

## Making ORomance Easier

Difficulty and convenience options are available in the ORomance Plus MCM.

Higher difficulty-modifier values actually make social interactions easier:

* **0:** intended progression; difficult during the early game;
* **30:** slightly easier;
* **60:** accessible to lower-level characters;
* **90:** most NPCs become approachable very early.

For the intended Krosis experience, start with the configured default settings and adjust them only if the system feels unnecessarily restrictive.

# Combat Guide

Krosis uses modern action combat based on MCO and **For Honor Reforged**.

Combat is built around deliberate attacks, target lock, stamina management, timed defense, poise damage, directional attacks, guard breaks, and follow-up strikes.

Button mashing is usually less effective than observing the enemy and choosing the correct response.

## Basic Principles

### Lock On

Target lock is particularly important in duels.

Several For Honor Reforged mechanics, especially special follow-up attacks, require you to be locked onto the opponent.

Locking on also makes directional movement and precise attacks much easier to control.

### Light Attacks

Normal attacks are your basic combo tools.

Use them to:

* deal reliable damage;
* maintain pressure;
* continue attack chains;
* reduce enemy poise gradually;
* prepare the enemy for a parry or knockdown.

### Power Attacks

Power attacks deal more damage and poise damage but consume more stamina and leave you vulnerable if used carelessly.

Different movement directions can produce different attacks.

### Directional Attacks

Hold a movement direction while attacking to perform directional moves.

For example:

* left movement + attack;
* right movement + attack;
* backward movement + power attack.

Side attacks are not merely cosmetic. They can continue combos, manage the enemy’s poise, and interact with directional combat effects.

## Guard Break

A **backward power attack** acts as the dedicated guard-break move.

To perform it:

1. lock onto the enemy;
2. hold backward movement;
3. use a power attack.

Against a blocking opponent, the guard break forces them into a sliding block reaction and creates an opportunity for a follow-up attack.

Against an opponent who is not blocking, it applies the temporary **Unstable** effect. If their poise is depleted while Unstable is active, they can be knocked down.

Guard break itself deals reduced direct damage, so it should be used as a setup tool rather than as a primary attack.

## Follow-Up Attacks

After a successful parry, guard break, or supported knockdown, quickly perform another attack.

You may follow up with either:

* a normal attack;
* a power attack.

Follow-ups cost less stamina and can deal increased damage.

You must be locked onto the target for these attacks to activate correctly.

## Poise

Poise represents an enemy’s ability to withstand impacts.

Attacking reduces poise. When it reaches zero, the opponent may stagger or become vulnerable to a knockdown.

Do not always empty the poise bar immediately. Sometimes it is better to lower it and then finish the sequence with a parry, guard break, or another special interaction.

## Parrying

A well-timed defensive action can parry an incoming attack.

Successful parries can interrupt the enemy, damage their poise, and create an opportunity for a follow-up strike or knockdown.

Enemies cannot be repeatedly knocked down without restriction. Special knockdowns have cooldowns, represented by status icons above the target.

During a cooldown, the enemy may temporarily resist another knockdown or receive modified damage.

## Timed Blocking

Blocking at the correct moment is substantially more effective than holding block indefinitely.

A well-timed block can:

* reduce incoming damage;
* disrupt enemy pressure;
* open a parry opportunity;
* conserve resources compared with passive blocking.

Holding block constantly is dangerous against enemies capable of guard breaking.

## Dodging

Dodging is useful for avoiding committed attacks, repositioning, and leaving an unfavourable engagement.

However, dodging repeatedly without managing stamina can leave you unable to attack, block, or escape afterward.

Use dodges to avoid a specific threat rather than as a permanent defensive state.

## Directional Combat Icons

Some enemies can gain empowered directional attacks.

An icon above the enemy indicates the relevant direction and attack type. These empowered attacks may deal additional health damage or drain stamina.

You can remove the enemy’s directional enhancement by striking from the corresponding direction.

For example, an enemy’s empowered left heavy effect can be countered with your own left power attack.

When an enemy is blocking, you may first need to apply the guard-break vulnerability before the directional effect can be removed.

## Fighting Groups

For Honor-style combat is strongest in one-on-one encounters, but Skyrim frequently places you against several enemies.

When outnumbered:

* avoid standing in the middle of the group;
* use terrain and doorways;
* keep enemies in front of you;
* reposition instead of completing every combo;
* eliminate weaker targets first;
* use followers, shouts, magic, and crowd control;
* disengage when your stamina is low.

Do not remain permanently locked onto one target while another enemy attacks from behind.

## Enemy Behaviour

Enemies use SCAR-supported movesets and can access many of the same mechanics as the player.

Depending on the encounter, they may:

* perform combos;
* dodge;
* parry;
* break your guard;
* use directional attacks;
* punish missed attacks;
* perform follow-ups.

Treat humanoid opponents as active combatants rather than stationary damage targets.

## In-Game Combat Help

Krosis includes Better Help Menu integration for For Honor Reforged.

Open Skyrim’s Help menu and look for entries beginning with:

**Advanced Combat**

These pages explain combat icons and advanced mechanics with illustrations.

# Legacy of the Dragonborn

Legacy of the Dragonborn, commonly shortened to **LOTD**, turns the entire playthrough into a long-term collecting and exploration campaign.

The museum is located in Solitude, near the Blue Palace.

Krosis contains extensive LOTD integration and supports thousands of museum displays from Skyrim, Creation Club content, and additional quest mods.

## Starting the Museum

After the initial Krosis museum-building notification has appeared, play normally until you are ready to visit Solitude.

Enter the museum and speak with **Auryen Morellus**, the curator.

He introduces the museum and starts the opening Legacy questline. From this point onward, the museum becomes a central hub for your playthrough.

You do not need to rush directly to it, but visiting it reasonably early is recommended.

## What Should Be Collected?

Collect anything that appears unique or unusual:

* named weapons;
* named armor;
* Daedric artifacts;
* Dragon Priest masks;
* quest rewards;
* books;
* gemstones;
* fossils;
* paintings;
* archaeological fragments;
* unusual clutter;
* items from supported quest mods.

When uncertain, take the item and check it at the museum later.

## Do Not Sell Unique Items Carelessly

Many items can be displayed directly or used to create replicas.

Avoid selling or discarding named and unusual items until you know whether they belong in the museum.

The Curator’s Companion and the list’s inventory icons help identify items that:

* have an available museum display;
* have already been displayed;
* are still required;
* can be replicated.

Pay attention to the museum-related icons shown in the inventory.

## Displaying Items

The museum provides sorting and display systems that place supported artifacts into their correct exhibits.

Deposit items through the designated museum storage and sorting containers rather than manually searching every room for a display.

Once an item has been registered, you may often retrieve the original and leave a replica on display.

## Replicas

Some unique artifacts can be reproduced at the Replica Workbench.

This allows you to:

* keep using the original item;
* place a replica in the museum;
* recover displays for artifacts that were previously sold or surrendered, when supported.

Not every object can be replicated, and some replicas require the original item to have been obtained or registered first.

## Display Count

Your total number of museum displays is not merely cosmetic.

Many Legacy quests and events become available after reaching specific display-count milestones.

As the museum grows, you will unlock:

* new museum quests;
* rewards;
* additional rooms and features;
* major story events;
* the Explorer’s Society;
* expeditions and archaeological content.

Do not attempt to rush every display milestone. Legacy is designed to unfold throughout an entire playthrough.

## The Safehouse

The museum eventually provides access to a large player home called the Safehouse.

It contains:

* crafting stations;
* extensive storage;
* displays;
* beds and living areas;
* facilities for followers and family;
* replica and archaeology tools;
* Craftloot support.

The Safehouse can serve as your main base once it becomes available.

## Craftloot

Craftloot allows crafting materials stored in supported containers to become temporarily available when using crafting stations.

This reduces the need to carry every ingot, ingredient, leather strip, and crafting component at all times.

After crafting, unused materials are returned to storage.

## Explorer’s Society

Later in the Legacy storyline, you can establish the Explorer’s Society.

The guild provides:

* recruitable specialists;
* archaeological expeditions;
* excavation quests;
* new facilities;
* additional Legacy storylines.

This content unlocks gradually as the museum and main Legacy questline progress.

## Recommended LOTD Playstyle

The best way to experience Legacy is to treat the museum as the record of your adventures rather than as a checklist that must be completed immediately.

A good routine is:

1. explore and complete quests normally;
2. collect unique items along the way;
3. periodically return to the museum;
4. sort new discoveries;
5. create replicas where necessary;
6. speak with Auryen and museum staff;
7. follow new Legacy quests when they appear.

Krosis contains a very large amount of supported content, so completing every museum display in one playthrough is neither required nor expected.

# Final Advice

Krosis is designed as a long-form playthrough.

Take your time, read the new menus, check the MCM descriptions, and allow its systems to develop naturally.

Remember the three most important rules:

1. **Do not touch anything at the start of a new game until the museum-build notification appears.**
2. **In combat, lock on, manage stamina, and use backward power attacks to break an enemy’s guard.**
3. **Do not sell unusual or unique items before checking whether the museum needs them.**
